# Client Brief — Chasm Bridge & Filament Command Center

Welcome to the **Chasm Bridge Charity & Filament (Pty) Ltd Command Center**. This guide is designed to help you navigate, understand, and use this tracking tool as we roll out the foundational phases of your digital presence.

---

## 🌟 Why this Tracker was Initiated

In digital project rollouts, it is easy for lines to get blurred between what is currently being built, what belongs to ongoing maintenance, and what is planned for future expansions.

This command center was initiated to serve as a **single source of truth** for all project stakeholders. Specifically, it was designed to:
1. **Prevent Scope Creep**: Maintain a clear boundary between **Phase 1 (included rollout)**, the **Retainer (keep-it-running digital care)**, and **Phase 2 / Phase 3 (future scale-ups)**.
2. **Provide Real-Time Visibility**: Allow team members and stakeholders to see exactly what is in progress, what is waiting on client input, and what is ready for launch, without requiring complex username/password logins.
3. **Ensure Launch Security**: Act as a digital "safety gate" (via the **Launch Readiness** list) to ensure no brands go public before their security, domain, and email systems are fully configured and approved.

> [!NOTE]
> **Real-Time Synchronization:** The tracker is built on top of a live database (Supabase). When changes are made by anyone on any device, the updates broadcast instantly and redraw on all other active screens automatically—no page refreshes required.

## 🗺️ Tour of the Command Center Views

Use the sidebar navigation to switch between these focused views:

### 1. 📊 Dashboard
* **What it is**: Your high-level control panel.
* **Key Features**: Live status counters (Completed, In Progress, Blocked, etc.) and a **Phase 1 Progress Bar** reflecting how close the foundational rollout is to 100% completion.

### 2. 🗂️ Task Command Center
* **What it is**: The master grid containing every detailed task (Strategy, Branding, Domain, Landing Pages, Social Setup, and Next Actions).
* **Key Features**: Inline dropdowns to change status and owners, date inputs, and notes modals.

### 3. 🛡️ Phase 1 Scope
* **What it is**: A breakdown of the core project deliverables.
* **Key Features**: Visual checklists outlining exactly what is included (e.g. logo design, static pages, profile setups) and what is not included in the current phase to keep project delivery transparent.

### 4. 📚 Client Assets
* **What it is**: Your homework list.
* **Key Features**: Details the files, copy, handles, and assets needed from the client side (e.g., logo preferences, text copy, reference letters) to unlock development.

### 5. 🚀 Launch Readiness
* **What it is**: The pre-flight checklist.
* **Key Features**: A progress bar and set of checks that must be set to "Done" before going live.

## 🛠️ How to Use and Modify the Tracker

Editing is enabled for authorized coordinators. Follow these simple steps to make updates:

### Step 1: Select an Active Editor
* On the bottom of the sidebar, locate the **Active Editor** dropdown.
* Select who is making the update (e.g., *Ndumiso*, *Dr. Rudy*, *Monique*, *Jazmin*).
* Alternatively, if you open a Notes Modal without selecting an editor, you can select your name directly inside the modal popup to instantly unlock editing.

### Step 2: Make Inline Changes
* **Status / Owner**: Click on the status pill or owner name in any row/card to select a new option from the dropdown menu.
* **Due Date**: Click on the date field. A native **Calendar Date Picker** will appear, allowing you to select dates easily.
* **Priority**: Click on the priority badge to change the urgency (`High`, `Medium`, `Low`).

### Step 3: Add Notes & View History
* In the table, click the **Notes & History** button on any row.
* A large overlay modal popup will slide open:
  * **Left Panel**: Type status notes, request details, or define the **Next Action**. Click **Save Note Update** to write to the log.
  * **Right Panel**: Browse the **Change Log & Audit Trail** to see a history of who changed what, when they changed it, and what the previous values were.
